# About Me

## My Journey

From an early age, I was fascinated by two things, numbers and technology. Mathematics gave me the thrill of solving problems, while technology sparked my curiosity about how the world could be transformed through innovation. Over time, I realized that the real magic happens when the two come together. This realization guided every step of my academic and professional journey.

## A Path of Resilience

I was born on March 10, 2000, in Gujrat, Pakistan. I began my schooling at Raza School, Gujrat, where I studied up to class 8. For matriculation, I joined Government Municipal Model High School for Boys, studying Mathematics, Physics, Chemistry, and Biology. I secured **918 out of 1100 marks**, performing well academically. Many people encouraged me to pursue medicine, but my passion for Mathematics led me in a different direction.

I enrolled in **FSc Pre-Engineering** at Government Zamindar Postgraduate College, Gujrat, and scored **819 out of 1100**. While most students leaned toward Biology, I consciously chose Mathematics. During this period, I discovered my aptitude for teaching and began teaching Mathematics at **Ali Science Academy**. That experience planted the seed of a lifelong passion for education.

Initially, I planned to pursue engineering and was even selected for a university program. Financial constraints forced me to reconsider, so I opted for a more affordable option, a **BSc in Mathematics and Physics** at Zamindar College. My academic journey was interrupted when the COVID-19 pandemic struck Pakistan, delaying my plans for higher education.

## The Turning Point: MSc and Data Science

After restrictions were lifted, the government began phasing out MSc programs in favor of the international four-year BS system. Only a few universities still offered MSc programs, including **Government College University, Lahore (GCUL)**. Determined not to abandon my goals, I moved to Lahore and completed my **MSc in Mathematics** with a **CGPA of 3.37/4.0**.

My MSc years proved to be a turning point. I strengthened my academic foundation, gained confidence, sharpened my problem-solving skills, and grew socially. More importantly, I was introduced to **Data Science**. Fascinated, I began learning **Python** through online resources and YouTube tutorials. Soon, I explored **Machine Learning**, discovering how it perfectly bridged Mathematics and technology, my two lifelong passions.

During my time at GCUL, I remained socially active. I was a member of the **Chawla Mathematics Society**, where I helped organize seminars and workshops. I also joined the **Blood Donor Society**, volunteering in blood donation drives and awareness campaigns.

## Career and the Quest for Knowledge

After graduation, I achieved my dream of becoming a teacher, but I set a new goal, pursuing a **PhD**. Teaching stayed close to my heart, but I also aspired to build a career in research and technology. I started teaching at a school after completing my MSc. While teaching was fulfilling, it soon became clear that it was not financially sustainable in Pakistan. Meeting basic expenses was difficult, and supporting a family through teaching alone seemed unrealistic.

Around this time, my earlier exposure to Data Science resurfaced. Combined with financial challenges, it motivated me to explore technology more seriously. I began learning Python independently while continuing to teach, holding on to my love for Mathematics and education.

I later enrolled in an **Artificial Intelligence (Machine Learning and Deep Learning)** training program at **Minhaj University, Lahore**, offered by **NAVTTC**. This marked my formal entry into programming. Alongside this, I completed multiple online courses from **Coursera**, including:

- Data Science Math Skills (Duke University)
- Discrete Mathematics (UC San Diego)
- Mathematical Thinking (Stanford University)
- Algebra (Johns Hopkins University)

I also worked on practical projects such as:
- Brain Tumor Detection using CNN
- Credit Card Fraud Detection
- Diabetes Classification
- CIFAR Image Classification
- AI-Based Career Selection Chatbot

These projects helped me merge strong mathematical foundations with real-world AI applications.

## Professional Experience

I then joined **InsightSol Technologies** for a one-month remote internship. My role involved training two machine learning algorithms using Python. While the exposure was limited, it provided my first structured experience with ML workflows and project execution.

After the internship, I continued teaching at **Hajveri Lyceum School**, where I taught Mathematics to grades 5 through 9. Teaching kept me financially stable and allowed me to refine my communication skills by simplifying complex ideas for young learners.

## The Road Ahead

Alongside teaching, I began preparing to study abroad. I took the **IELTS**, achieving an overall **7.0 band score**:
- Listening: 8.0
- Reading: 7.5
- Writing: 6.5
- Speaking: 6.0

Despite applying for multiple international scholarships, my **2+2 degree structure (BSc + MSc)** led to repeated rejections. This was a discouraging phase, but it strengthened my resolve. I adopted a step-by-step strategy, completing an MS in Pakistan first, then reapplying abroad for a PhD.

Currently, I am working in a challenging and rewarding role at **Meissasoft**. This full-fledged internship goes beyond coding. I gain real industry exposure by:
- Practicing OOP through a **Chess Game Engine** and **Ride-Sharing Simulation**
- Solving **Data Structures and Algorithms** problems on HackerRank
- Designing **SQL databases**
- Building a **CRUD-based Student Management System** using **FastAPI and SQLAlchemy**

Unlike my earlier internship, this role provides deep insight into industry practices, project structures, database design, API development, and application testing. Here, I truly feel part of the tech ecosystem.

## The Road Ahead

I was admitted to the MS Data Science program at UET, marking a new phase in my academic journey. The program strengthened my commitment to AI research and advanced learning. During my first semester, I studied new Data Science topics, expanded my technical skills, and achieved a CGPA of 3.68, reflecting consistent progress.

At the same time, I continued my internship at Meissasoft, where I gained structured exposure to software development practices, system design, and collaborative workflows. While working there, I received a separate remote job opportunity in an R&D role focused on voice agent testing and development.

In this role, I build and evaluate voice agents using Pipecat, while researching ways to improve efficiency and performance. The work involves understanding how real conversational systems operate, including Text-to-Speech (TTS), Speech-to-Text (STT), and Large Language Models (LLMs). I also explored prompt engineering and studied how AI agents reason, respond, and adapt in real environments.

This experience moved my learning from theory to applied research. Instead of only implementing models, I now analyze behavior, reliability, and interaction quality, skills directly aligned with my long-term goal of contributing to advanced AI systems.

## Conclusion

My journey has been a gradual transformation rather than a single decision. Mathematics developed my problem-solving mindset. Teaching strengthened my ability to explain ideas clearly. Artificial Intelligence connected my curiosity with real-world impact.

Financial limitations, academic interruptions, and repeated rejections did not stop my progress. They refined my direction. Today I am pursuing advanced studies while actively working with practical AI systems, bridging research and industry together.

My goal is to earn a PhD, contribute meaningful research in AI and Data Science, continue teaching, and build intelligent solutions that improve real-world life.

The path is still unfolding, and I am moving forward with clarity and purpose.
